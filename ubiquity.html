Challenge

In July 2017, the company I worked for acquired a marketing automation company called Ubiquity. The app was pretty old, and came with about 10 years of unpaid technical debt.

It was challenged by an inconsistent design language, making the app difficult to use or understand. It had to deal with the legacy of many tacked-on features created through sales driven development, and was caught between a strategic crossfire; too small to compete with Adobe Experience Cloud and Marketo, and too complex to compete with small business marketing offerings like MailChimp and CampaignMonitor.

Discovery & Research
We started with an audit and stock-take of every feature, as well as listening to users about what they needed to do their customer communications & marketing.


Exploration & Feature Specification
The data told us that the most damaging issue customers experienced was the feeling of being overwhelmed. As a new user seeing the platform for the first time, you were bombarded with jargon, and the process of setting up a new account was not clear. Given that most feature development was directed at existing customers who had their power users, product development tended to prioritize feature development over onboarding. We remedied this issue as we designed and tested new onboarding features for users, by running guerrilla SUS testing quickly and cheaply to identify glaring usability issues throughout the onboarding process.
