Challenge

In July 2017, the company I worked for acquired a marketing automation platform called Ubiquity.

The app came with about 10 years of unpaid technical debt, and product development had slowed to a crawl. It was also initially challenged by an inconsistent design language, making it difficult to onboard new users. As a team, we also had to contend with the legacy of many tacked-on features created through a history of sales-driven development.

It was caught between a strategic crossfire. The platform wasn't mature enough to compete with Adobe Experience Cloud or Marketo, and too complex to compete with small business marketing offerings like MailChimp and CampaignMonitor.

We also had to compete with the fact that the revenue generated by managed services (ie: our company manages the software for you) meant that there was a stronger business case for feature development over refactoring the platform.
